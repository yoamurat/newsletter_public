Oil Hits $116 as Hormuz Closure Sparks Energy Crisis

Brent crude rose above $116 as oil prices have risen nearly 60% since the Iran war began, with President Trump threatening to obliterate Iran's energy infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz isn't reopened by April 6. More than 3 mb/d of Middle East refining capacity has shut due to attacks, while Gulf oil producers have reduced production as regional storage fills up. For a Paris-based process engineering consultancy, this crisis creates massive opportunities in emergency energy infrastructure assessments, refining optimization studies, and strategic project reviews as clients scramble to secure alternative supply chains and assess geopolitical risks to their facilities.
